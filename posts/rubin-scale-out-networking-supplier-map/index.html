Rubin Scale-Out Networking Supplier Map

Spectrum-X, Quantum-X, and LinkX all belong to Rubin’s scale-out domain, but they occupy different layers. The investable supplier clues differ sharply between the shipping LinkX pluggable path and NVIDIA’s newer co-packaged photonics architecture.

Research Scope Public NVIDIA, Broadcom, Lumentum, and Coherent disclosures on Rubin-era scale-out networking, LinkX transceivers, and Spectrum-X / Quantum-X photonics.
01

Key Findings

Stack distinction
Platforms vs. links

Spectrum-X and Quantum-X are networking platforms; LinkX is the cable and transceiver family inside those platforms.

Current TAM
LinkX broader today

LinkX covers DACs, AOCs, and pluggable optics across a larger currently deployed base.

Frontier architecture
Photonics newer

Spectrum-X / Quantum-X Photonics are the newer CPO switch architectures for top-end AI factories.

Best supplier clues
AVGO, LITE, COHR

Broadcom is the strongest public LinkX component clue; Lumentum and Coherent are the clearest photonics ecosystem clues.

Bottom line: LinkX is the visible pluggable interconnect path in Rubin documentation. Spectrum-X / Quantum-X Photonics are the higher-value architectural direction. For public equities, the cleaner evidence currently points to Broadcom around LinkX components and to Lumentum, Coherent, TSMC, and Fabrinet around switch-side photonics.

02

Scale-Out Taxonomy

Spectrum-X / Quantum-X and LinkX are both scale-out, but they are not substitute products. LinkX is the physical interconnect layer used by the networking platforms.

06

TAM And Timing

LinkX has the larger served TAM today

LinkX spans a wider deployed base: DACs, AOCs, pluggable optics, and cables across Spectrum, Quantum, ConnectX, and BlueField systems. It is the current monetized scale-out interconnect layer.

Confidence: inferred from product scope and availability, not a published NVIDIA TAM figure.

Photonics is the higher-value frontier architecture

Spectrum-X / Quantum-X Photonics compress the switch-side optics problem into co-packaged photonic engines, external lasers, packaging, and fiber attach. The revenue base is less mature, but the strategic value per frontier switch is higher.

Confidence: architecture-driven inference supported by NVIDIA’s CPO positioning.

07

Rubin Technology Read

Visible Rubin NVL8 module

MMS4X00-NM-HGX is an 800G LinkX optical transceiver documented for Rubin NVL8 links into Spectrum switches.

Newest Ethernet architecture

NVIDIA says Spectrum-X Ethernet Photonics is integrated into Rubin, making it the newest disclosed Ethernet-side scale-out architecture.

InfiniBand counterpart

Quantum-X800 / Quantum-X Photonics is the InfiniBand-side frontier direction for scale-out AI-factory networking.

08

Investment Conclusion

The public evidence is cleaner for NVIDIA photonics suppliers than for Credo-style Rubin LinkX exposure.

For LinkX, NVIDIA / Mellanox remains the module vendor-of-record and Broadcom is the strongest external component clue. For Spectrum-X / Quantum-X Photonics, NVIDIA has explicitly named a wider ecosystem, with Lumentum and Coherent standing out on lasers and optics, TSMC on silicon / packaging, and Fabrinet on manufacturing.

The practical underwriting split is: use LinkX for current scale-out interconnect TAM and photonics for the frontier CPO architecture. Do not treat CRDO or MRVL as confirmed Rubin LinkX suppliers unless a future disclosure pins them to a specific NVIDIA part or design win.
